Tenancy Resources

This guide contains government resources and tools on tenancy and renter rights for residents of the Vancouver area with resources from all three levels of government.

Note: This guide is intended for residents of the City of Vancouver, not the Metro Vancouver Regional District. For information specific to your area, please consult the official website of your individual municipality.

    City of Vancouver – Renter Supports and Protections

    This City of Vancouver webpage provides an overview of municipal bylaws, housing policies, and renter support programs designed to protect tenants in Vancouver. Find current policies like the Single Room Accommodation (SRA) By-law, the Tenant Relocation and Protection Policy , and updated tenant protections due to redevelopment.

    The source addresses common renter issues, including eviction processes, mutual agreements, unsafe housing conditions, and illegal rental units. It connects users to provincial tenancy information through the Residential Tenancy Branch, and directs users to external legal and community services such as the Tenant Resource and Advisory Centre (TRAC), Renting It Right, and Access Pro Bono.

    Financial assistance options are also included, such as BC Housing Rental Assistance Programs and the Vancouver Rent Bank, for emergency rental assistance loans. The page additionally notes municipal licensing requirements for all residential rental properties in Vancouver.

    This official website serves as a central access point for tenant supports in Vancouver, combining municipal policies, provincial legislation, and referral pathways to legal and financial support services. This resource is useful for identifying your rights as a renter and finding up-to-date information on eviction, relocation, redevelopment, and unsafe rental conditions protections. Navigate the site’s dropdown menus and select relevant links, using this site as a gateway tool to access legal information and support services.

    City of Vancouver - Rental Housing Inventory

    This inventory page from the City of Vancouver provides a list of rental housing options across Vancouver, including low-end market (set at a discount to appraised market rents) rentals, non-market housing on City-owned land (developed in partnership with government and non-profit providers), and City-owned market rental housing.

    Users can browse rental options categorized by neighbourhood, apartment type (e.g. studio, 1-bedroom suite), and current and upcoming housing developments. The page also links to the Non-Market Housing Inventory and an index map that can be filtered by project or building sponsor, as well as applications for government-supported housing.

    This resource is valuable for renters seeking affordable and government-supported housing options and information on current and upcoming housing developments. It also provides contact information to express interest in the listed housing options.

BC Laws – Residential Tenancy Act

The Residential Tenancy Act details the primary legislation that governs rental housing in British Columbia. These laws outline the legal rights and responsibilities of landlords and tenants alike.

This legal document includes information about tenancy agreements, rent increases, evictions, dispute resolutions, and tenant protections. Importantly, it is the primary legal framework governing rental housing in British Columbia and takes precedence in cases where municipal bylaws conflict with its provisions. Access a full list of BC tenancy laws and rules here.

This website can be navigated using a table of contents, which details the sections of the Residential Tenancy Act. Users can use the “Find” function of their browser to search for terms of interest, such as “eviction” or “rent increase” to find the laws of interest. Within the document, there are hyperlinks to other Acts that the document refers to, which users can click upon to read more on other legal documents. These features are especially useful to identify specific rights and responsibilities of a tenant or landlord, such as during a tenancy dispute.

Government of British Columbia - Residential Tenancy Branch (RTB)

The Residential Tenancy Branch administers the Residential Tenancy Act and Manufactured Home Park Tenancy Act in British Columbia, with the goal of ensuring landlords and tenants have successful tenancies. The website provides comprehensive guidance on all stages of renting, along with access to various services such as dispute resolution.

This website offers official and up-to-date information directly from the body that enforces tenancy law in British Columbia. It connects users to provincial tenancy information and provides encyclopedia-like guidance on basic knowledge required to start, navigate, and end tenancies. Crucially, the Residential Tenancy Branch website provides a formal dispute resolution process that ends with a legally binding decision. The site also contains tools such as rent increase calculators and multilingual support for over 200 languages, making it accessible for a wide range of users.

This homepage of the website contains a list of the most commonly accessed services and information topics. There is also a search bar to locate topics in other pages of the website. Upon navigating to an information page, there is a menu on the left side-bar that includes the main sections of the website, along sub-sections of the section that the user is currently within. There are also forms and documents available on various pages, such as a fillable and legally binding Residential Tenancy Agreement Form. When using a service such as applying for a dispute resolution, users can login using their BCeID information, and track the status of their case online.

BC Human Rights Tribunal - Tenancy and Human Rights

The BC Human Rights Tribunal’s guide on tenancy in British Columbia explains how human rights law can apply to rental housing. It outlines what constitutes discrimination during tenancies, and identifies protected grounds for tenants, such as race, disability, and family status. Furthermore, the webpage provides guidance on filing complaints and seeking remedies under the Human Rights Code, which is a legally binding Act of British Columbia.

This resource is essential for tenants experiencing housing discrimination, which can include denial, harassment, or eviction based on the protected grounds highlighted under the Human Rights Code. It can also be used to assess if a user’s situation meets the legal threshold for discrimination and what evidence is required to warrant a discrimination complaint. This can help inform tenants of whether or not to pursue additional legal action during cases of tenancy disputes.

This website can be navigated using a table of contents that organizes the information into 3 sections, including what evidence needs to be shown to make a discrimination complaint, who can be named as the respondent of the complaint, and what defenses are justifiable in the face of complaints. Common words and phrases that are used in human rights are highlighted as hyperlinks to other pages with definitions. This site can be complemented by other resources and legal documents such as the Residential Tenancy Act to determine appropriate processes and legal action for tenants and landlords alike.

Tenancy Dispute Resolution Decisions

This resource from the official website of the Government of British Columbia provides a reference tool for searching past decisions to determine whether the Residential Tenancy Branch (RTB) has already heard and decided a similar issue.

Decisions are organized by type, including direct request decisions, participatory hearing decisions, special application decisions, monetary orders, and administrative penalties, and are defined for ease of use.

The decision search tools are separated by decision date (e.g., direct request decisions are available in a separate search tool for decisions issued on or after December 21, 2022, while the residential tenancy decision archive contains direct request decisions issued before December 20, 2022).

To use the posted decision search tool, search by dispute properties, free text, or decision ID, then browse the results and download the relevant decision: Posted Decisions Tool .

The Residential Tenancy decision archive can be searched using free text and sorted by best match or most recent: Decision Search Archive .

For efficient searches, users can also apply Boolean operators to narrow results: Boolean Search Guide .

Guidance Document on the Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA): Private Sector Landlords and Tenants

This resource from the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of British Columbia explains how privacy laws apply to landlords and tenants under the Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA) .

This document outlines the rules that govern the collection, use, and disclosure of personal information in landlord-tenant relationships, including the obtaining and withdrawal of consent.

This guidance is useful for understanding privacy rights in rental relationships before establishing tenancy and during tenancy. It clarifies what is considered personal information (e.g., name, date of birth, physical characteristics, income) and clearly outlines landlords’ obligations under PIPA using plain language and an accessible question-and-answer format.

Renting Your First Apartment – Financial Consumer Agency of Canada (FCAC)

This webpage authored by the Financial Consumer Agency of Canada provides basic and practical information for renting an apartment or a house in Canada. The purpose of this webpage is to give renters, particularly first-time renters, a practical overview of the financial, legal, and personal considerations involved in renting.

This resource is written in plain language, making the guide accessible and easy to understand for most readers. It is useful because it explains the full picture of renting in simple terms, from making the decision on whether to rent to making a rental agreement and living with roommates. It is a starting point for users to learn more about the process of renting, make informed and financially-conscious decisions, and avoid common mistakes.

This website includes a table of contents that explains the main sections of the website, that users can click to quickly navigate to the sections. Users can use the “Find” function of their browser to search for terms of interest, such as “budget” or “renter’s insurance” to find the topics of interest on the webpage. Furthermore, at the end of each section, the website includes links to other webpages from the Canada Mortgage And Housing Corporation that explain the topic in more detail. While information of the website is broad, it explicitly tells the user to familiarize themselves with their provincial and municipal rights and responsibilities, given that tenancy laws vary across the country.

Housing Data and Demographics – Statistics Canada (2021 Census)

This resource from Statistics Canada’s 2021 Census provides housing and demographic data for the Greater Vancouver Regional District. It includes information on housing tenure, affordability, suitability, offering a statistical overview of housing conditions in the region.

Note: This resource reflects data for the Greater Vancouver Regional District, which includes 21 municipalities, Electoral Area A, and scəẃaθən məsteyəxʷ (Tsawwassen First Nation), rather than the City of Vancouver alone.

This is a user-friendly resource that provides statistical context on recent housing issues, trends, and renter demographics that affect Greater Vancouver residents.
